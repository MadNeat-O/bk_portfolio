ABOUT ME

Brad Korman is a multifaceted filmmaker, photographer and entrepreneur. A native New Yorker, ever since a young age, Brad has loved film and music and when he's not working on client projects you can find him playing his guitar or training Muay Thai.

While attending Oberlin College, Brad was a Multi-Media Mentor as part of a pilot program to help teach technical skills to other students including teaching workshops and holding office hours in the editing lab. During summers and winter term he would freelance in camera and grip departments on indie films in NYC
After graduating with a Cinema Studies Major and Photography Minor, Brad was awarded a Cinema Studies Production Fellowship, which included coordinating student and faculty productions and maintaining the shooting studio and editing lab in the newly renovated Danny DeVito and Rhea Perlman Cinema Studies Center for Media Education and Production.

During this fellowship, Brad started an independent recording studio and production company out of his house in Oberlin. After the school year ended, he found a studio in Brooklyn and started the first iteration of Remarque Creative.
